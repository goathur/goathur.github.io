AQ Sensor Emulation & Visualization

Skills Used

Instrumentation Testing
Technical Writing
Communication

Programs Used

MATLAB
Microsoft Excel
TrakPro
As a part of the Air Quality (AQ) team under the Cobian-Iñiguez Fire Laboratory, my goal was to study air quality during wildfire events to better understand local-scale exposure to wildfire smoke. To do this, I tested and utilized sensors that are specifically used for scanning and identifying the types of particulate matter in the air by setting these sensors up around the UC Merced campus.
Initial tests were done using a high-end particulate matter tracking sensor called the DustTrak to test the capabilities of accurately measuring particulate matter variation within the atmosphere. This was tested through running almond harvesting simulations, and then visualizing the results to clearly see each classification of particulate matter.
Due to the cost of high-quality air monitoring sensors such as the DustTrak, we want to be able to see if we can find sensors that can produce the same effect as the DustTrak, but at a lower price. After determining the thresholds of the DustTrak, the settings that were used on it were applied onto a low-cost sensor called the Dylos Air Quality Monitor to emulate the accuracy of the DustTrak.
