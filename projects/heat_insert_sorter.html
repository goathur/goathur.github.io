Heat Insert Sorter

Skills Used

Computer-Aided Design
Finite Element Analysis
Dimensioning/Tolerancing
Data Visualization

Programs Used

ANSYS
Siemens NX
MATLAB
OrcaSlicer
As a general problem within assembly shops across various industries, there have been multiple instances where components with size varients, such as heat inserts, are sorted into the wrong bin. This creates waste, as due to difficulties in re-sorting these types of components by hand, it would mean turning important resources for manufacturing into waste. To resolve this problem, I developed a sifting mechanism to sort heat-set inserts for 5 different sizes through filtering incorrect insert sizes. This was designed as an attachment for a vortex spinner, which would also make the functionality of this instrument multi-purpose rather than only being used as a liquid substance mixer.
Through material analysis, mechanical design, and rapid iteration, creating this mechanism has facilitated in reducing the cost of wasted inserts and has increased the efficiency of sorting through the inserts by 93%.
