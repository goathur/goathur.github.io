TRW-Solar Cooker Kitchen

Skills Used

Computer Aided Design
Finite Element Analysis
Data Visualization
Technical Writing

Programs Used

ANSYS
SolidWorks
Microsoft Excel
For my engineering capstone (senior design) project, I teamed up with a group of two mechnical engineering students and a bioengineering student under the research architect, Joel H. Goodman, to design a built-in stove that utilizes solar rays to cook food. This was done by designing a reflector structure similar to that of a Compound Parabolic Concentrator (CPC) reflector used to capture energy for solar panels.
I creating an efficient way of modeling the cookers funnel parts by designing molds to mass produce the funnel components, both by ensuring the clay funnels structural integrity while curing on the molds and reducing the amount of waste by making them reusable. I also researched an ideal material for the solar cookers funnel by performing studies on various clay material compositions based on their cation exchange capacity and testing their properties through using thermal analysis methods.
Our work on CPC reflector designs used in solar cooking was published under the UC Merced Undergraduate Research Journal (URJ). You can read more about it here!
